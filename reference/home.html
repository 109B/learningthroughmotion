About us

Learning Through Motion: Growing Toward Independence


At Learning Through Motion, we believe every child deserves a rich, inclusive learning experience that nurtures both their educational and personal development. That’s why we specialise in delivering cross-curricular learning through sport, designed especially for children with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND).

Our unique programmes blends physical activity with classroom topics such as Mathematics, creating engaging, active learning environments where SEND students can thrive. By using sport as a dynamic teaching tool, we foster not only academic growth but also confidence, coordination, communication, and social interaction.

Through carefully designed, inclusive sports experiences, we provide opportunities for students to build confidence, communication, resilience, and key life skills. Whether it’s improving fine and gross motor skills through team games, strengthening sensory systems through our Sensory Redevelopment activities, or reinforcing classroom subjects through physical challenges, our approach makes learning fun, meaningful, and accessible.

We work closely with schools, educators, and support staff to tailor our sessions to each child’s individual needs and alternative learning approaches—ensuring that every child is seen, supported, and empowered. By integrating cross-curricular learning into every session, we meet each child where they are, supporting their development in an environment that celebrates their strengths and uniqueness.

At Learning Through Motion, inclusion isn’t an add-on—it’s the foundation. We’re passionate about breaking down barriers and opening up new pathways for learning, helping every child reach their full potential through the power of sport.

Our ultimate goal is to give every student the best possible chance at leading an independent, fulfilling life. We celebrate every small win, nurture every unique ability, and walk alongside each student on their journey toward becoming confident, capable, and independent.

Every child deserves the chance to succeed—at their own pace, in their own way, and in an environment that celebrates who they are.
